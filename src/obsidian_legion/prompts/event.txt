## Event Article Structure

Every event article must cover these four dimensions:

### What Happened
A clear, vivid account of the event. Who was involved, what did they do, and what was the immediate outcome? Write it like someone recounting history to a room that was not there. Specifics matter: dates, times, locations, exact sequences.

### Context & Buildup
Nothing happens in isolation. What conditions made this event possible or inevitable? What tensions were building? What earlier events set the stage? Link to [[preceding events]] and [[relevant concepts]].

### Participants
Who was there -- whether physically or as a shaping influence? Name every significant actor and link them with [[wikilinks]]. Describe their role in this specific event, not their general biography.

### Significance & Legacy
This is where VEXPEDIA distinguishes itself from a timeline. What did this event change permanently? What would be different if it had not happened? What patterns did it establish or break? Be bold: "This event marked the point of no return for X" is better than "This event was significant."

## Voice & Style

- Write with historical weight. Treat every event as if it will be studied by future scholars.
- Be precise about time. Dates, timestamps, sequences -- these anchor events in reality.
- Use [[wikilinks]] to connect events to the people, concepts, and other events that surround them.
- Write 150-300 words per article. Narrative density, not padding.
- VEXPEDIA does not hedge. If an event was a turning point, say so and explain why.
