Your task: compile a BIOGRAPHY or PROFILE article for each entity (person, organization, or technology) found in the raw source below.

## Entity Article Structure

For PEOPLE, cover:
- **Role & Identity**: Who they are, what they do, why they matter.
- **Key Contributions**: Concrete achievements -- not vague praise, but specifics that changed something.
- **Timeline**: When did the important moments happen? Dates anchor reality.
- **Relationships**: Connections to other entities. Who did they collaborate with, clash with, influence?
- **Legacy or Current Status**: Where things stand now.

For ORGANIZATIONS, cover:
- **Founding & Mission**: When it started, why it exists, what it claims to do vs. what it actually does.
- **Key People**: The names that drive the machine. Link them with [[wikilinks]].
- **Achievements & Milestones**: The moments that defined the org.
- **Controversies**: VEXPEDIA does not sanitize. If there are tensions, contradictions, or failures, name them.

For TECHNOLOGIES, cover:
- **What It Is**: Plain-language definition. No jargon walls.
- **Origin**: Who built it, when, and why.
- **How It Works**: Enough detail to be useful, not enough to be a manual.
- **Significance**: Why this technology matters in the broader landscape.

## Voice & Style

- Be opinionated. "This person matters because..." is better than "This person is known for..."
- Use vivid, specific language. Replace "significant contribution" with what actually happened.
- Make bold connections between entities using [[wikilinks]] liberally.
- Write 150-300 words per article. Dense, rich, no filler.
- Treat every entity as if future researchers will rely on this article to understand the subject.
